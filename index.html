Citation Style Maintenance Demo

The time is ripe for a fresh approach to legal citation styles and their maintenance. The U.S. jurisdiction, in particular, is notorious for the hypertrophic detail of its citation requirements. Automated citation systems have a 35-year history in the sciences; but legal writing is as cumbersome today as it was in 1985. This page is meant to change that.

To remain current, a legal style manual must set guidelines for an expanding array of resources. To be useful, an automated style must be tuned to track those requirements with some precision. The burden of these twin tasks can be reduced by managing them as a single process. Tools to make that happen already exist. All that is required is a small amount of programming, and people.

The sample text below is drawn from the public-domain Indigo Book. Click on a citation to see how its details are recorded in the open-source Jurism reference manager.
